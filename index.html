      Data & methodology

      • Grades — official UCSD grade distributions, 2015–2026, aggregated per course × professor across quarters. The bar shows the full spread A/B/C/D/F, plus W (withdraw) and P/NP; Avg GPA is the historical mean.
      • Offered this term — matched against the live Fall 2026 WebReg catalog ( course codes). An Offered tag means the course runs this term.
      • Course page link — every offered course carries a Course page ↗ link straight to its WebReg / TSS enrolment page (UCSD login required); in My Schedule, Open all course pages launches them all at once so you can enrol in one pass.
      • This term's instructor (FA26) — pulled from the WebReg Course Planner catalog (SahirSSharma), which lists each Fall 2026 section's professor. On offered rows we show the actual current instructor(s); a Teaching now tag means the row's past instructor is the one teaching it this term, so their grade history is directly relevant. rows are tagged this way. Neither source carries an instructor id, so names are linked by a precision-first ladder: normalise order/accents, handle compound surnames (Massimiliano Di Ventra ↔ Di Ventra, Massimiliano) and Mc/apostrophe spellings, match on a first or middle initial (Ryan Wagner ↔ Wagner, Timothy Ryan), and auto-alias genuine renames by shared course (Paul Cao teaches the same courses Cao, Yingjun has taught). A professor teaching at UCSD for the first time has no grade history, so their name is marked 0× and sorted after the returning instructors.
      • Schedule builder — add offered courses to My Schedule to lay out lectures/discussions/labs on a weekly calendar (with conflict detection) and see the buildings on a campus map. Section times, rooms and instructors come from the same FA26 catalog; selections are saved in your browser. Each course card also shows its final exam (FI) date & time for the selected section group, and overlapping finals are flagged as conflicts. Export to Calendar downloads an .ics file (Apple Calendar, Google Calendar, Outlook) with weekly recurring events for the Fall 2026 term (Sep 24 – Dec 4) plus one-time final-exam events, campus holidays excluded and Pacific-time DST handled. Open all course pages launches every scheduled course on WebReg in its own tab, so you can enrol in one pass — this needs pop-ups allowed for this site (browsers block multiple tabs by default), and you should be signed into TSS first.
      • Meeting-time filter — the 🕐 filter keeps only courses that can actually fit your week: in at least one section group, every required meeting type (lecture / discussion / lab) must have a section inside your chosen days & hours. The weekly grid mode works like When2Meet — paint the hours you're free. Sections with TBA times always count as fitting.
      • RMP — RateMyProfessors (school 1079), name-matched (~80% hit). Difficulty lower is easier; again is would-take-again %. Blank means no match.
      • Grad backfill — courses with no grade data (mostly 200+) are filled in reverse from professors' RMP course history, shown as RMP only. Those RMP scores are the professor's overall rating, not specific to that course.
      • Caveat — history is a guide, not a guarantee; instructors and grading policies change. Fewer times taught = less reliable (use the Taught ≥ filter).
